# 20 Fun Two-Choice Prompts (Image-Based)

Each prompt is designed to feel BuzzFeed-like (simple, playful, two image choices) while still mapping cleanly to one MBTI-style axis: **E/I**, **N/S**, **T/F**, **J/P**.  
I’m listing which axis each prompt targets, plus what each choice *leans toward*.

---

## E vs I (Extraversion vs Introversion) — 5 prompts

1) **Your ideal Friday night is…** *(E/I)*  
   - A: crowded house party / dance floor *(E)*  
   - B: cozy room + headphones + snack *(I)*

2) **At a new event, you’re drawn to…** *(E/I)*  
   - A: the loud group laughing *(E)*  
   - B: the quiet corner with one person chatting *(I)*

3) **Your phone battery is at 5%. You choose…** *(E/I)*  
   - A: call someone anyway *(E)*  
   - B: airplane mode and disappear *(I)*

4) **You get a free hour. You’d rather…** *(E/I)*  
   - A: go out and “see what happens” *(E)*  
   - B: stay in and do your own thing *(I)*

5) **When something exciting happens, you…** *(E/I)*  
   - A: tell people immediately *(E)*  
   - B: keep it to yourself for a bit *(I)*

---

## N vs S (Intuition vs Sensing/Observant) — 5 prompts

6) **You’re choosing a gift. You pick…** *(N/S)*  
   - A: something symbolic / inside joke *(N)*  
   - B: something practical they’ll use *(S)*

7) **You’re exploring a city. You follow…** *(N/S)*  
   - A: a random side street that “feels interesting” *(N)*  
   - B: a list of top-rated places *(S)*

8) **You’re watching a movie. You care more about…** *(N/S)*  
   - A: the theme / message / meaning *(N)*  
   - B: the details / realism / what actually happened *(S)*

9) **Your desk looks like…** *(N/S)*  
   - A: chaotic but “I know where everything is” *(N)*  
   - B: neat, labeled, and organized *(S)*

10) **You trust a decision more when it’s based on…** *(N/S)*  
   - A: a gut feeling / pattern you notice *(N)*  
   - B: clear evidence / what you can see *(S)*

---

## T vs F (Thinking vs Feeling) — 5 prompts

11) **A friend asks for advice. You respond with…** *(T/F)*  
   - A: the honest solution, straight up *(T)*  
   - B: comfort first, then advice *(F)*

12) **In an argument, you value…** *(T/F)*  
   - A: being correct and consistent *(T)*  
   - B: keeping the relationship okay *(F)*

13) Someone cancels plans last minute. Your first reaction is…** *(T/F)*  
- A: “Okay — what happened? Give me the reason.” *(T)*  
- B: “Are you okay? Don’t feel bad.” *(F)*  

14) **A group project is failing. Your instinct is to…** *(T/F)*  
   - A: assign roles and fix the system *(T)*  
   - B: check in on how everyone’s doing *(F)*

15) **You respect someone more when they are…** *(T/F)*  
   - A: smart and efficient *(T)*  
   - B: kind and thoughtful *(F)*

---

## J vs P (Judging vs Perceiving/Prospecting) — 5 prompts

16) **Packing for a trip looks like…** *(J/P)*  
   - A: checklist + folded outfits *(J)*  
   - B: throwing stuff in last minute *(P)*

17) You’re going out to eat with friends. You’re the one who…** *(J/P)*  
- A: checks the menu ahead of time and already has a plan *(J)*  
- B: decides on the spot based on the mood *(P)*  

18) **When you start a project, you…** *(J/P)*  
   - A: outline first, then execute *(J)*  
   - B: jump in and figure it out *(P)*

19) **Your to-do list is…** *(J/P)*  
   - A: clean, realistic, and satisfying to complete *(J)*  
   - B: a living creature that keeps growing *(P)*

20) **You get an invitation for tomorrow. You feel…** *(J/P)*  
   - A: happy because you can plan it *(J)*  
   - B: nervous because your mood might change *(P)*

---

## Note on images (so it stays “BuzzFeed”)
For each option, I can use:
- simple icons/emojis,
- small illustrations,
- or photos (e.g., “party” vs “cozy room,” “checklist” vs “messy suitcase”).
