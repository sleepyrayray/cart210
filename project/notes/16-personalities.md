# Quick Reference: The 16 Personalities (16Personalities Framework)

These notes list the 16 personality types as described by 16Personalities, with a short “what makes them unique” snapshot. I’m keeping the descriptions brief so I can later decide how (or if) I want to remix these ideas inside my quiz project. ## Analysts (NT)

### INTJ — Architect
Imaginative and strategic, they like long-term plans and tend to approach life like a system that can be optimized.  [oai_citation:1‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### INTP — Logician
Curious and invention-oriented, they enjoy exploring complex ideas and understanding how things work at a deep level.  [oai_citation:2‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ENTJ — Commander
Bold, decisive organizers who naturally take charge and push toward goals, often by building structure around people and projects.  [oai_citation:3‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ENTP — Debater
Quick-minded challengers who love intellectual sparring, new angles, and testing ideas by poking at them from every side.  [oai_citation:4‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

## Diplomats (NF)

### INFJ — Advocate
Quiet but intense idealists who care about meaning and impact, often driven by a strong internal vision of how things *should* be.  [oai_citation:5‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### INFP — Mediator
Gentle, values-driven personalities with a poetic streak; they’re motivated by authenticity and helping causes that feel important.  [oai_citation:6‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ENFJ — Protagonist
Charismatic “people movers” who inspire others and tend to read emotions well, often stepping into mentorship or leadership roles.  [oai_citation:7‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ENFP — Campaigner
Energetic and imaginative free spirits who chase possibilities, connect quickly with others, and bring contagious enthusiasm.  [oai_citation:8‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

## Sentinels (SJ)

### ISTJ — Logistician
Reliable and practical, they value duty, consistency, and clear rules—often becoming the person everyone depends on.  [oai_citation:9‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ISFJ — Defender
Warm protectors who show care through loyalty and effort; they’re often quietly supportive and strongly grounded in responsibility.  [oai_citation:10‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ESTJ — Executive
Direct and organized administrators who like clear roles and efficient systems—good at coordinating people and getting things done.  [oai_citation:11‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ESFJ — Consul
Social, caring helpers who pay attention to people’s needs and community harmony, often acting as the glue in groups.  [oai_citation:12‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

## Explorers (SP)

### ISTP — Virtuoso
Hands-on experimenters who learn by doing; they’re tool-focused problem-solvers who stay calm in practical challenges.  [oai_citation:13‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ISFP — Adventurer
Flexible, aesthetic, and experience-oriented; they value freedom and tend to express themselves through taste, vibe, and creativity.  [oai_citation:14‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ESTP — Entrepreneur
Energetic risk-takers who act fast, read situations sharply, and enjoy high-stimulus environments where decisions matter.  [oai_citation:15‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)

### ESFP — Entertainer
Spontaneous and lively, they bring fun and momentum to social spaces and often thrive when they can engage people directly.  [oai_citation:16‡16Personalities](https://www.16personalities.com/personality-types)
